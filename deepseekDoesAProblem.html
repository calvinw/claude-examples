Certainly! Let's break down the steps to solve the problem of finding the probability that a value xxx is less than 53 in a normal distribution with a mean (μ\muμ) of 42 and a standard deviation (σ\sigmaσ) of 12.

Step 1: Understand the Problem

We want to find the probability that xxx is less than 53 in a normal distribution with:

  • Mean (μ\muμ) = 42

  • Standard deviation (σ\sigmaσ) = 12

This probability corresponds to the area under the normal curve to the left of x=53x = 53x=53.

Step 2: Calculate the Z-Score

The Z-score standardizes the value xxx to a standard normal distribution (mean = 0, standard deviation = 1). The formula for the Z-score is:

z=x−μσz = \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma}z=σx−μ​

Plugging in the values:

z=53−4212=1112≈0.9167z = \frac{53 - 42}{12} = \frac{11}{12} \approx 0.9167z=1253−42​=1211​≈0.9167

Step 3: Use the NORMSDIST Function

The NORMSDIST function in a spreadsheet (or a similar function in statistical software) gives the cumulative probability to the left of a given Z-score in a standard normal distribution.

Using the Z-score we calculated:

NORMSDIST(0.9167)≈0.8203\text{NORMSDIST}(0.9167) \approx 0.8203NORMSDIST(0.9167)≈0.8203

This means there's approximately an 82.03% chance that a value xxx is less than 53 in this normal distribution.

Step 4: Interpret the Result

  • To four decimal places, the probability is 0.8203.

  • Rounded to the nearest percent, it's 82%.
