The left-tail area and the right-tail area refer to different parts of the normal distribution curve, and understanding the distinction is important for interpreting probabilities in statistics.

Left-Tail Area:

  • The left-tail area is the cumulative probability or area under the curve to the left of a given z-value.
  • In simple terms, it represents the percentage of values in the distribution that are less than or equal to the z-value.
  • For example, if we find that the left-tail area for z=1.02z = 1.02z=1.02 is 0.8461 (or 84.61%), this means that 84.61% of the data in the normal distribution lies to the left of z=1.02z = 1.02z=1.02.

Right-Tail Area:

  • The right-tail area, on the other hand, is the area to the right of the same z-value.
  • It represents the percentage of values that are greater than the z-value.
  • In a standard normal distribution, the total area under the curve is 1 (or 100%), so the right-tail area is simply: Right-tail area=1−Left-tail area\text{Right-tail area} = 1 - \text{Left-tail area}Right-tail area=1−Left-tail area

For the example where z=1.02z = 1.02z=1.02:

  • The left-tail area is 0.8461, so the right-tail area is: 1−0.8461=0.15391 - 0.8461 = 0.15391−0.8461=0.1539 This means that 15.39% of the data lies to the right of z=1.02z = 1.02z=1.02, or in other words, 15.39% of values are greater than z=1.02z = 1.02z=1.02.

Visualizing the Difference:

  • The left-tail area corresponds to the shaded region to the left of the z-value, which is what we've calculated so far:

  • The right-tail area would be the complement of that, representing the region to the right of the z-value, which is unshaded in this image.

When Do We Use Each?

  • Left-tail area is used when you're interested in knowing the probability that a value is below a certain threshold.
    • Example: "What percentage of people have a test score lower than 70?"
  • Right-tail area is used when you're interested in knowing the probability that a value is above a certain threshold.
    • Example: "What percentage of people scored higher than 90 on the test?"

In summary, the left-tail area represents the probability of a value being less than or equal to a z-value, while the right-tail area represents the probability of a value being greater than that z-value. They are complementary areas, adding up to 1 (or 100%).
